In TherapyNotes, the patient intake process is designed to reduce duplicate documentation by carrying structured intake data forward into the clinical record and session note workflow.

The flow generally works like this:

Intake Forms Are Completed by the Patient
Patients complete digital intake paperwork through the portal before the first session. This may include:
Demographics
Emergency contacts
Insurance information
Consent forms
Presenting concerns
Mental health history
Substance use history
Medications
Screening tools (PHQ-9, GAD-7, etc.)
Trauma history
Treatment goals

That information becomes part of the patient chart immediately after submission.

Intake Data Populates the Patient Chart
The intake responses are stored in structured sections of the chart rather than as isolated PDFs only. Providers can reference:
Diagnoses
Medication lists
Allergies
Family history
Risk factors
Prior treatment history
Screening scores

inside the clinical documentation interface.

Initial Assessment Pulls from Intake Data
When the clinician opens the intake/diagnostic assessment note:
demographic fields auto-fill
insurance and referral information auto-fill
screening scores may import automatically
medication and history sections can be referenced or inserted
previously entered treatment goals may appear for editing

The clinician still must clinically validate and summarize the information rather than simply accept it blindly.

Subsequent Session Notes Reference Existing Clinical Data
In ongoing psychotherapy notes:
diagnosis lists persist
treatment plan goals remain linked
prior note content can be referenced
medication lists remain accessible
alerts, risk history, and screenings remain visible

TherapyNotes supports “copy forward” style workflows where portions of previous notes or treatment plans can be referenced to reduce repetitive entry.

Treatment Plans Feed Into Progress Notes
Treatment plan objectives are especially integrated into the session workflow:
clinicians can select active goals/objectives
interventions can align to those goals
progress toward goals can be documented directly inside the note

This creates continuity between:
Intake → Assessment → Treatment Plan → Progress Note → Outcome Tracking

Screening Tools Flow Longitudinally
If PHQ-9, GAD-7, or other measures are completed repeatedly:
scores trend over time
providers can compare prior assessments
outcome reporting becomes easier
medical necessity support is strengthened

This is one reason many behavioral health EHRs integrate intake forms tightly with clinical documentation.
