Yes. This is trying to replicate enterprise PM software like PracticeSuite or Athena, but for behavioral health private practices it creates cognitive overload.

You currently have:

* too many left-nav items
* duplicate concepts
* too many queue categories visible at once
* billing terminology clinicians do not understand
* multiple navigation systems competing simultaneously
* no hierarchy between “daily work” and “exception work”

The biggest issue:
You are exposing operational architecture instead of user workflow.

A biller thinks:

1. What needs attention right now?
2. Why is it stuck?
3. What action do I take?

Not:

* 999
* 277CA
* payer status
* clearinghouse trace
* duplicate review
* partial payments
* etc all at once.

You need:

* primary workflow queues
* progressive disclosure
* role-based simplification

The current layout feels like:
“Here are all database states.”

Instead it should feel like:
“Here is today’s work.”

BILLING
